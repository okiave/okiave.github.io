Agility: the metric of performance

At the core of Agile are a few basic beliefs that bring value to the end user often and in a repeatable manner.

  • Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
  • Working software over comprehensive documentation
  • Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
  • Responding to change over following a plan

How: Results over process

Producivity can stagnate when the steps to the final outcome become bottlenecked with checkboxes for the sake of having a checkbox. Here are a few examples of bottleneck checkboxes:

  • A passive team member in management doesn't like feeling left out and requires personal meetings often and on their schedule.
  • A specific less efficient technology/tool must be used because the owner invested a lot of capital and can't accept it not be utilized.
  • Established legacy systems/methods have senority and cannot be undermined. Ask permission first to try a different approach.
  • Department A is only allowed to contact customer about all aspects of their project because department A made first contact with customer and is in essence the expert to the customer for all things on their project.
  • No matter what, the bean counter department must approve.
